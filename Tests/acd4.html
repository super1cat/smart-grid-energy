Common Questions

What is a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)?

A PPA allows you to get solar installed at no upfront cost. You only pay for the electricity it generates, at a lower locked-in rate.

Do I own the panels?

In a PPA, the provider owns and maintains the system. You just enjoy the benefits of solar power without responsibility for upkeep.

How long does a PPA last?

A typical PPA lasts 20 to 25 years, depending on the agreement with the provider. After the term, the homeowner may have the option to purchase the system or renew the agreement.

What happens when my PPA term ends?

At the end of your PPA term, you can either purchase the system, renew the agreement, or have the system removed.

Can I transfer my PPA to a new homeowner?

Yes, most PPAs are transferable to new homeowners, allowing the new owners to benefit from the lower energy rates.

How does solar ownership work?

When you own the solar panels, you are responsible for maintenance but enjoy the full financial benefits of reduced electricity bills.

What are the benefits of owning a solar system?

Ownership provides long-term savings and energy independence, allowing you to keep any excess energy produced and earn credits for it.

What is the upfront cost of owning solar panels?

The upfront cost of purchasing solar panels can vary depending on the size of the system and any available incentives or rebates.

Do I need a special permit to install solar panels?

Yes, depending on your location, you may need a permit for installation. This is typically handled by the installer.

How do I maintain my solar panels?

Solar panels generally require little maintenance. You should clean them periodically and ensure there are no obstructions blocking sunlight.

What is a grid provider?

Grid providers manage and deliver electricity to your home. They are responsible for maintaining the infrastructure for the energy grid.

Can I choose my grid provider?

In some regions, you can choose your grid provider based on pricing, reliability, and services offered.

What happens if my grid provider has an outage?

If your grid provider experiences an outage, they will work to restore power. You may also benefit from a backup solar battery system during these times.

What are the costs associated with grid providers?

Grid providers typically charge for the electricity they deliver, along with service fees. Rates can vary depending on your location and provider.

Can I switch grid providers?

In some regions, you can switch grid providers. Check with your local utility to see if this option is available in your area.

What is net metering?

Net metering is a system where solar panel owners can sell excess electricity back to the grid for credits on their utility bills.

How can I lower my electricity bill?

Consider switching to solar energy, as it significantly reduces your monthly electricity bill by using the sun's power for your home.

What is time-of-use billing?

Time-of-use billing is when electricity rates change depending on the time of day. Typically, electricity is cheaper during off-peak hours.

Can I use solar power without a battery?

Yes, you can use solar power without a battery. However, without a battery, excess power will be sent to the grid, and you may not be able to store it for later use.

What is the cost of electricity from solar panels?

The cost of electricity from solar panels is lower than traditional utility rates, and it varies depending on the size of the system and your energy consumption.
