Common Questions

PPA Basics

What is a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)?
A PPA allows you to buy electricity from solar panels installed on your roof without owning the equipment. You simply pay for the power you use.
How long does a PPA last?
Most PPAs last 20–25 years, with predictable, slow annual increases.
What happens when my PPA ends?
You may have the option to renew, remove the system, or buy it outright. We'll help you choose what works best.

Owning Your Solar System

Is it better to own or lease solar panels?
That depends on your goals. Ownership can offer long-term savings and potential incentives, while a PPA provides savings without the equipment investment.
Can I finance my system?
Yes. We offer financing options to make ownership simple and accessible.

Electric Companies – PG&E, SDG&E, and more

Can I still be connected to the grid?
Yes. You'll remain connected for backup power when solar isn’t producing enough.
Do I still get a utility bill?
You may see a small bill from your electric company, mostly for basic grid service.

How Solar and Batteries Work

How do solar panels power my home?
Panels capture sunlight and convert it into electricity for your home through an inverter.
What does the battery do?
Your battery stores extra energy and powers your home during outages or at night.

General Electricity Questions

Is solar energy reliable?
Yes, especially with battery backup. Solar works even when the grid goes down.
What are the main benefits of switching to solar?
Lower energy costs, greater control, cleaner energy, and protection from rising rates.
