Common Questions

What is a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)?

A Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) is a contract that allows you to install solar panels with no upfront cost. You only pay for the electricity it generates, typically at a lower rate than your utility provider.

How long does a PPA last?

A typical PPA lasts 20 to 25 years, depending on the agreement with the provider. After the term, the homeowner may have the option to purchase the system or renew the agreement.

What happens when my PPA term ends?

When your PPA term ends, you may have the option to buy the solar system at a discounted rate or continue with a new agreement.

What are the benefits of owning a solar system?

Owning a solar system provides long-term savings, energy independence, and the ability to sell excess energy back to the grid.

How does solar ownership work?

When you own the solar panels, you are responsible for maintenance but enjoy the full financial benefits of reduced electricity bills.

Can I finance the purchase of solar panels?

Yes, there are various financing options available, including solar loans and tax incentives to reduce upfront costs.

What is a grid provider?

Grid providers manage and deliver electricity to your home. They are responsible for maintaining the infrastructure for the energy grid.

Can I choose my grid provider?

In some regions, you can choose your grid provider based on pricing, reliability, and services offered.

What is net metering?

Net metering is a system where solar panel owners can sell excess electricity back to the grid for credits on their utility bills.

How can I lower my electricity bill?

Consider switching to solar energy, as it significantly reduces your monthly electricity bill by using the sun's power for your home.
