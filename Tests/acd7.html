Common Questions

Power Purchase Agreement Basics- How solar electricity works without owning the system
What is a Power Purchase Agreement?›
A PPA lets you buy electricity from solar panels installed on your home without owning the equipment. You get predictable, lower-cost power with no upfront cost for the system.
Who maintains the solar system?›
The company that provides the electricity installs, maintains, insures, and even replaces the batteries as needed—so you don't have to worry about any of it.
Owning Your Solar System- Questions about buying or financing your system
Can I own my solar system instead?›
Yes! Some homeowners prefer to buy or finance their system. We’ll help you compare both options to see what fits best for you.
Electric Companies – PG&E, SDG&E, and others- Learn more about your current utility provider
What’s the difference between this and my current electric company?›
Instead of paying PG&E or SDG&E, you're simply switching to a provider that gives you electricity from solar panels installed on your roof—with better rates and built-in battery backup.
How Solar and Batteries Work- What to know about panels, energy, and storage
How do solar panels power my home?›
They convert sunlight into electricity during the day, which powers your home directly or charges your backup battery for later use.
General Electricity Questions- Basics about energy use and reliability
Will I still be connected to the grid?›
Yes. Your home remains connected for backup or extra energy use—but most of your power will come from your own roof.
