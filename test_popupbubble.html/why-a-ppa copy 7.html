Why a PPA - Power Purchase Agreement?

The Electric Bill Just Keeps Climbing

Homeowners across California are feeling more pressure than ever.

What used to be a manageable monthly bill is now growing faster than anyone expected.

Read More

Meanwhile, utility companies like PG&E are reporting record profits — even as they continue raising rates. Families are left wondering:
How much worse is this going to get?

What many don’t realize is that they do have a choice!

A PPA lets qualified homeowners switch electricity providers and power their homes with on-site solar— at a lower, stable rate per kilowatt-hour.

It’s a low-friction way to take control of your energy costs — and most people have never even heard of it.

Switching Is Surprisingly Simple

When your home qualifies, the PPA electric company handles everything — they install, own, insure, and maintain the solar equipment.

It's a true bill swap. You pay nothing upfront.

Read More

Once your system is turned on, your old utility bill drops to a basic connection fee, typically between $15 and $20.

From that point forward, you’ll begin receiving a new electric bill —
but this time, it’s for the power produced on your own roof.

It’s basically like the company installs a mini electric plant right on your home, simply selling you the energy at a lower rate than what you are paying now.

Backup Power Without the Burden

Power outages are becoming more common — but when the grid goes down, your lights, fridge, and essentials stay on.

Most PPAs include a home battery system at no added cost — keeping your home powered and your family safe.

Read More

One of the biggest benefits of a PPA is what happens when the battery needs replacing. Whether that’s in six years or twenty, the PPA electric company handles the replacement — at no cost to you.

Your monthly bill stays exactly the same.

No interruptions. No surprises.


It’s reliable energy you can count on — without the stress of maintenance or replacement costs.

Save Now — and Keep Saving

Many homeowners see 10% to 40% savings right from the start.

With lower bills, families finally have room to plan ahead — from building savings, to upgrading the home, to booking a well-earned vacation.

Read More

Unlike traditional electric companies that raise rates frequently and at random, your PPA rate stays steady — with only a small annual increase for maintenance.

Since you don’t own the equipment, there’s nothing to fix, replace, or worry about.

And because the power is generated right at your home, it’s more efficient, with less waste and lower carbon impact.

The sun goes up, your bills go down and the earth keeps spinning around!

What’s the Catch?

There really isn’t one—but not every home qualifies. Things like roof shading, layout, and usage patterns all matter.
